Burger

What is a burger?

A burger is a hamburger meat patty sliced into thin, uncooked strips, often served on a bun or roll. It is traditionally made with ground beef, pork, or chicken, often seasoned with ketchup, mustard, relish, and various other ingredients. Ingredients

  • Buns: 1 (12 oz) loaf
  • Patty: 1 (4 oz) ground beef or pork or chicken
  • Cheese: 1/2 cup shredded cheese (e.g., cheddar, mozzarella, or feta)
  • Lettuce: 1 (1/2)
  • Tomatoes: 1/2
  • Onions: 1/2
  • Pickles: 1/2
  • Bacon: 1/2
  • Butter: 1/4 cup
  • Sauce: 1/4 cup (such as ketchup, mustard, or mayonnaise)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the buns on a baking sheet and lightly oil the bottom of the pan.
  3. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef or pork or chicken, and cook until browned and cooked through
  4. Add the shredded cheese, lettuce, and any other toppings to the pan and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the lettuce is tender.
  5. Once the cheese is melted and the lettuce is tender, stir in the ketchup, mustard, and any other desired toppings. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the burger is cooked through and the cheese is melted and the lettuce is tender.
