Spaghetti

What is a spaghetti?

Spaghetti is a type of pasta that is cooked in a sauce made of tomatoes, onions, garlic, and other ingredients. It is commonly served with meatballs, sautéed vegetables, or even as a side dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds ground beef or pork or chicken
  • 1/4 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Romano cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Gorgonzola cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Provolone cheese
  • 1/2 cup fresh lettuce, chopped
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/2 cup mustard
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup red onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the onion and red onion in olive oil until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped fresh parsley and cook for an additional 1 minute.
  4. Add the chopped lettuce, ketchup, and mustard, and toss to combine.
  5. Stir in the shredded mozzarella, Parmesan, Parmigiano-Reggiano, Romano, Gorgonzola, Monterey Jack, and Provolone cheeses.
  6. Add the ground beef or pork or chicken, and cook for 3-4 minutes, until browned and cooked through.
  7. Transfer the spaghetti to a baking dish and cover with foil or parchment paper.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the spaghetti is tender.
  9. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh parsley and a sprinkle of Parmigiano-Reggiano.
